My Cameras

4 cameras in rotation, each with its own quirks and character.

Kodak Duoflex II

620 / 6x6 Medium Format Kodak Anastigmat f/4.5 1/25 to 1/200 + B 25-400 1950s

The Kodak Duoflex II is a beautiful vintage TLR that makes shooting medium format feel tactile and intentional. Its simple controls and iconic design are perfect for slow, thoughtful film work, and I love the character it brings to square-frame portraits and street scenes.

Agfamatic 2008

Format 110 film Year Introduced 1976 Lens Agfa Color-Agnar, fixed focus Shutter Fixed speed, selenium-metered auto exposure Flash Magicube compatible

The Agfamatic 2008 is my go-to camera for spontaneous moments. Its pocketable size makes it easy to carry everywhere, and the 110 film format gives a unique, nostalgic look to the shots.

Why I Love It

  • Ultra-compact, drop-in cartridge loading
  • Simple point-and-shoot operation
  • Charming 70s design and vintage aesthetic
  • Perfect for street photography and travel

Pentax SP2

Format 35mm Year Introduced 1971 Lens Mount M42/Praktica Shutter Speed 1/1000 to 1 second ISO Range 25-1600

The Pentax SP2 is my main 35mm camera for more serious photography. It’s a robust and reliable SLR with excellent build quality and a wide range of compatible lenses.

Why I Love It

  • Solid metal construction
  • Bright viewfinder
  • Accurate light meter
  • Compatible with many M42 mount lenses
  • Simple, intuitive controls
  • Classic 70s design

My Experience

I found this camera at a local camera shop in excellent condition. It came with a 50mm f/1.8 Takumar lens, which is incredibly sharp. The camera has never let me down, and the light meter is surprisingly accurate for its age. The M42 mount gives me access to a wide range of affordable, high-quality lenses.

Rollei B35

Format 35mm Year Introduced 1969 Lens Rollei Triotar 40mm f/3.5 Focus Zone/scale focus Shutter Speed 1/30 to 1/500 second

The Rollei B35 is one of the smallest full-frame 35mm cameras ever made, and it still feels incredibly well built for its size. No rangefinder, just zone focusing and a built-in light meter - it forces you to slow down and guess distances, which is part of the fun.

Why I Love It

  • Genuinely pocketable, solid German engineering
  • Sharp little Triotar lens
  • Great for quick, unobtrusive street shots
  • Looks as good as it shoots
