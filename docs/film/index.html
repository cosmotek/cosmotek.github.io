Learnings

Film Learnings

Getting Started

  1. Start Simple: Begin with a basic 35mm camera like the Canon AE-1. It’s user-friendly but still teaches you the fundamentals.
  2. Learn the Basics: Understand exposure (aperture, shutter speed, ISO) before moving to more advanced techniques.
  3. Practice Loading Film: Practice loading and unloading film in daylight until you’re comfortable doing it in the dark.

Development Process

Black and White Development

  • Use Ilford ID-11 developer at 1:1 dilution
  • Standard development time: 7-8 minutes at 20°C
  • Always use fresh fixer
  • Wash thoroughly after fixing

Tips for Better Results

  1. Meter Carefully: Take your time with light metering. It’s better to bracket exposures when unsure.
  2. Keep Notes: Document your settings and development times for each roll.
  3. Handle with Care: Always handle film by the edges and keep it away from heat.
  4. Be Patient: Film photography is a slow process. Embrace it.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  1. Not checking the film rewind knob before opening the camera
  2. Forgetting to set the correct ISO
  3. Rushing the development process
  4. Not cleaning the camera regularly
