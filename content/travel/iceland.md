Iceland feels otherworldly — a landscape of jagged cliffs, thundering waterfalls, and quiet geothermal pools. I explored the Ring Road in sections, stopping at highlights like Seljalandsfoss, Skógafoss, and the black sand at Reynisfjara. The contrast between icy glaciers and steaming hot springs is striking; take a guided tour onto a glacier and end the day relaxing in a hot lagoon.

Practical tip: pack warm layers and waterproof shells — weather changes quickly. Renting a car gives flexibility to chase the light, and visiting outside peak season yields solitude at many sites. If you're lucky, the northern lights make any cold night unforgettable.
