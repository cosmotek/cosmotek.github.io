Cusco is a vibrant highland city where colonial architecture sits atop Incan stone foundations. I spent days acclimatizing, wandering narrow streets, and visiting nearby ruins before taking the train to Machu Picchu. Markets like San Pedro offer vibrant textiles and local flavors, and altitudes require patience — stay hydrated and avoid overexertion early on.

Consider a day trip into the Sacred Valley to visit Ollantaytambo and Pisac, and book Machu Picchu or the Inca Trail well in advance during peak season.
