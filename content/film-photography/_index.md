# Film Photography

I've been exploring film photography as a creative outlet and a way to slow down and appreciate the world around me. There's something magical about the process - from loading the film to developing it in the darkroom.

## My Equipment

![My film kit](/images/film-gear/kit-flatlay.jpg)

- Pentax Spotmatic SP2 (35mm SLR)
- Agfamatic 2008 (110 film)
- Rollei B35 (35mm viewfinder)
- 55mm lens
- Various M42 mount lenses and filters

![55mm lens on the Pentax SP2](/images/film-gear/lens-macro.jpg)

## Film Stocks

A rotating mix depending on the light and the mood:

- Kodak Gold 200
- Fuji 400
- Ilford HP5 (B&W)
- Lomography Color Tiger 200
- Lomography Color Gold 200

## Development Process

I develop my own black and white film at home using a Paterson tank kit, with a sous vide heater to keep the chemistry at a consistent temperature:

- Ilford ID-11 developer
- Ilford Rapid Fixer
- Ilford Stop Bath

## Tips for Beginners

1. Start with a simple camera - the Canon AE-1 is a great beginner-friendly option
2. Learn the basics of exposure (aperture, shutter speed, ISO)
3. Don't be afraid to make mistakes - film photography is a learning process
4. Consider developing your own film - it's rewarding and cost-effective
