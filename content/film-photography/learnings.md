# Film Photography Learnings

Here are some key lessons I've learned along my film photography journey. I'll continue to update this page as I learn more.

## Technical Learnings

### Exposure
- Always bracket your shots when in doubt
- The light meter in vintage cameras can be fooled by bright backgrounds
- Negative film has more latitude than slide film
- Overexposing slightly is often better than underexposing

### Development
- Temperature control is crucial for consistent results
- Agitation technique affects grain and contrast
- Fresh chemicals make a big difference
- Keep detailed notes for each roll

## Creative Learnings

### Composition
- The square format of medium format cameras forces you to think differently
- Slowing down leads to better compositions
- Sometimes the best shot is the one you didn't take
- Look for leading lines and patterns

### Film Choice
- Different films have different personalities
- Black and white film is great for learning
- Color film can be expensive, so make each shot count
- Expired film can yield interesting results

## Practical Tips

### Equipment
- Keep your cameras clean and well-maintained
- Always carry extra batteries
- A light meter app on your phone is a great backup
- Invest in a good scanner or find a reliable lab

### Workflow
- Label your film rolls immediately after shooting
- Keep a detailed shooting log
- Organize your negatives carefully
- Back up your scans in multiple places
